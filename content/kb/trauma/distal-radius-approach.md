# Volar Approach to the Distal Radius (Henry)

> Placeholder — replace with faculty-reviewed content before pilot. Intended for PGY-3+ depth.

## Indications
- Displaced extra-articular distal radius fractures
- Intra-articular fractures requiring anatomic reduction
- Failed closed reduction / inadequate alignment on splinting

## Approach Steps (overview)
1. Longitudinal incision over the FCR tendon, extended distally to the wrist flexion crease
2. FCR retracted ulnarly; enter the flexor carpi radialis tendon sheath
3. Divide the floor of the FCR sheath to expose the pronator quadratus (PQ)
4. Divide/elevate PQ off the radial border → subperiosteal dissection exposes fracture
5. Plate placement: contour to the volar radial cortex, confirm with fluoroscopy
6. Critical: ensure screws do not penetrate dorsal cortex or articular surface

## Key Structures at Risk
- **Radial artery**: ulnar to FCR tendon — retract radially
- **Palmar cutaneous branch of median nerve**: transverse branch ~5cm proximal to wrist
- **FPL tendon**: lies deep to FDP; prominent in thin patients

## Fluoroscopic Landmarks
- Lateral: restore volar tilt (avg +11°)
- PA: restore radial inclination (~23°) and radial height (~12mm)
- Skyline view: confirm dorsal screw clearance
